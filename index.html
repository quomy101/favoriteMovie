The Witcher

#1 in TV Shows Today

The Witcher is a fantasy drama series about Geralt of Rivia, a monster hunter for hire who struggles to find his place in a world where people often prove more wicked than the beasts. The show has well-developed characters, top-notch world-building, and some of the best action scenes on television. It is also beautifully shot and full of humor, heart, and suspense.

What was lost

The Witcher season 2 episode 3, "What Was Lost," is a complex and emotionally-charged episode that explores the themes of loss, grief, and power. The episode follows several different storylines, including Geralt and Ciri's training at Kaer Morhen, Yennefer's struggle to come to terms with her lost magic, and the political intrigue surrounding the mages of Aretuza. One of the most interesting parts of the episode is the way it explores the concept of loss. Geralt and Ciri both experience loss in different ways. Geralt loses Eskel, one of his closest friends and fellow witchers. Ciri loses her innocence and her sense of safety when she is forced to kill a leshy. Both characters are forced to come to terms with their losses in different ways. Another interesting part of the episode is the way it explores the theme of power. Yennefer's lost magic is a symbol of her lost power. She is no longer the powerful sorceress she once was, and she struggles to find her place in the world. The episode also explores the power dynamics between the mages of Aretuza. Tissaia is the de facto leader of the mages, but she is challenged by Vilgefortz, a powerful mage who is vying for control.
